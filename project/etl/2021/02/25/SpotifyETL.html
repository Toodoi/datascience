Cosine Similarity

Cosine similarity measures the similarity between two vectors by calculating the cosine of the angle between them. The similarity score ranges between 0 and 1 with the cosine of a 0 degree angle being equal to 1. This means that if two vectors are identical they will recieve a paired score of 1.

The math for cosine similarity is just the dot product of the two vectors divided by the product of the magnitude of each vector.

def cos_sim(a, b):
    dot_product = np.dot(a, b)
    norm_a = np.linalg.norm(a)
    norm_b = np.linalg.norm(b)
    
    return dot_product / (norm_a * norm_b)

features = df[['danceability', 'energy', 'key', 'loudness', 'acousticness', 'valence', 'tempo']]
norm = MinMaxScaler().fit(features)
vec_norm = norm.transform(features)

sim_list = []
idx_list = []

for first_vector in range(0, 105):
    for second_vector in range(first_vector, 104):
        sim = cos_sim(vec_norm[first_vector], vec_norm[second_vector+1])
        sim_list.append(sim)
        idx_list.append((first_vector,second_vector+1))

df_sim = pd.DataFrame({'Similarity' : sim_list, 'Indexes': idx_list})

There were 5,460 similarity pairs calculated and the distribution is plotted below. Most tracks had a relatively large cosine similarity score, probably because they are all taken from the same user.
